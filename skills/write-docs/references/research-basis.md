# Research Basis — Empirical Grounding per Principle

This skill is grounded in empirical research, not opinion. Each principle's basis below.

## Per-principle basis

**P1 — First insight in 10 seconds.** NNG — probability of leaving highest in first 10s. At 150 WPM technical reading speed, 10 seconds ≈ 25 words of actual reading. The first 50-80 words must be scannable to that point.

**P2 — Inverted pyramid.** NNG inverted pyramid study — comprehension improves when key facts precede context.

**P3 — One heading per 150-200 words.** NNG eye-tracking — Layer-Cake pattern fixates on headings/subheadings. F-pattern (worse) emerges when headings are absent or vague.

**P4 — Maximum 4 concepts per section.** Cowan's revision of Miller's Law — short-term memory capacity is 3-5 chunks, not 7±2.

**P5 — Progressive disclosure in 3 layers max.** Interaction Design Foundation research on progressive disclosure. Deeper nesting increases cognitive load and abandonment.

**P6 — Sentences average 14-20 words.** American Press Institute study of 410 newspapers. Directionality confirmed by modern readability research (Siteimprove, Hemingway).

**P7 — Paragraphs under 100 words.** NNG paragraph-position attention study. Cross-referenced with web writing research.

**P8 — Front-load information-carrying words.** NNG F-pattern eye-tracking — fixation density is highest on first 2 words of each line.

**P9 — Code or visual within first 2 screenfuls.** NNG scrolling study — first two screenfuls get 74% of viewing time; first screenful alone gets 57%.

**P10 — Every key concept gets a concrete aid.** Picture superiority effect (Medina/Brain Rules). Developers with comprehensive code examples were 73% more effective (arXiv study on crypto APIs).

**P11 — Bullet lists for 3+ parallel items.** NNG — bulleted list fixation rates exceed inline prose by significant margins across multiple eye-tracking studies.

**P12 — Zero promotional language.** NNG's only controlled A/B study of writing style — the most rigorous evidence in this skill. Objective alone +27%; concise + scannable + objective combined +124% (n=51).

**P13 — Concise above all.** Same NNG A/B study — conciseness had 2x the impact of scannability and 2x the impact of objectivity (+58%, the single highest-impact change measured).

**P14 — Time to first working result under 5 minutes.** The 15-Minute Rule (industry heuristic). Stripe's "7 lines of code," Vercel one-click deploy, Supabase instant database as exemplars. Note: the specific threshold is an industry convention, not a controlled study.

**P15 — Every page ends with a concrete next action.** NNG — dead-end pages have significantly higher exit rates than pages with clear forward navigation.

## Source summary

- **NNG eye-tracking studies** (1997-present): F-pattern, Layer-Cake pattern, paragraph attention decay, scrolling behavior, reading percentages.
- **NNG A/B study on writing style** (n=51): the only controlled experiment measuring impact of concise + scannable + objective writing (58%/47%/27%/124% usability improvement).
- **Chartbeat engagement data** (2023, network-wide): 28.1s average engaged time, declining year over year.
- **Cowan's working memory revision** (2001): 3-5 chunks, not Miller's 7±2.
- **American Press Institute sentence study** (410 newspapers): comprehension vs. sentence length curve.
- **arXiv code examples study**: developers 73% more effective with comprehensive examples.
- **Picture superiority effect** (Medina/dual-coding theory): 65% vs 10% retention at 3 days.

## Critical caveats

NNG's n=51 A/B study is small but unique — no other controlled test exists. The "15-Minute Rule" is an industry heuristic, not empirical. Chartbeat data skews toward publisher/news sites, not documentation specifically. All findings should be treated as directional with high confidence, not precise measurements.
